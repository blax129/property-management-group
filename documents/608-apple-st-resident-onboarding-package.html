If rent is not received by the 5th day of the month, Tenant shall pay a late fee of $50.00, plus an additional $10.00 per day thereafter until paid in full, not to exceed the maximum permitted by Arkansas law. Returned payment fees of $35.00 shall apply to any dishonored payment.

Rent and other charges shall be paid by method approved by Landlord or Property Manager. Tenants shall follow payment instructions provided in the Resident Move-In Handbook or by the Property Manager. Cash payments should be avoided unless pre-approved in writing.

If Tenant takes possession on a date other than the first day of a month, rent for that partial month shall be prorated at a daily rate of $1,050.00 divided by the number of days in that month, unless otherwise agreed in writing.

Article 4 — Occupancy, Use & Rules

4.1 Permitted Occupancy

The Premises shall be occupied only by the five (5) named Tenants listed in Section 1.2. No additional persons shall reside at the Premises for more than fourteen (14) consecutive days or more than twenty-one (21) days in any calendar month without Landlord's prior written consent. Overnight guests must comply with all Lease terms.

4.2 Residential Use

The Premises shall be used exclusively as a private residential dwelling. Tenant shall not use the Premises for any illegal purpose, nor for any business, commercial, or professional activity without Landlord's written consent.

4.3 Joint & Several Liability

Each Tenant is jointly and severally liable for the full performance of every obligation under this Lease, including rent, fees, damages, and all other charges. Landlord may enforce this Lease against any one or more Tenants without affecting the liability of the others. Internal arrangements among Tenants regarding rent splits do not affect their collective obligation to Landlord.

4.4 Quiet Enjoyment

Landlord covenants that Tenant, upon paying rent and performing all terms of this Lease, shall peacefully and quietly hold and enjoy the Premises during the term.

4.5 Rules & Regulations

Tenant shall comply with all rules in the Resident Move-In Handbook (incorporated by reference), all applicable federal, state, and local laws, and all HOA or subdivision rules if applicable. Tenant shall not:

  1. Create or permit nuisances, excessive noise, or disturbances affecting neighbors;
  2. Engage in illegal activity on or about the Premises;
  3. Smoke inside the Premises (unless otherwise agreed in writing);
  4. Store hazardous materials without authorization;
  5. Operate unauthorized businesses from the Premises;
  6. Allow vehicles to be repaired or stored on the lawn or driveway in violation of local ordinances.

4.6 Parking

Tenant may park passenger vehicles in the concrete driveway and designated areas. Commercial vehicles, inoperable vehicles, and RV/boat storage require prior written approval. See Resident Handbook for parking assignments among roommates.

4.7 Keys & Access Devices

Tenant shall not duplicate keys or access devices without Landlord's consent. All keys shall be returned at move-out. Lost keys may be replaced at Tenant's expense.

Article 5 — Maintenance & Repairs

5.1 Landlord's Responsibilities

Landlord shall maintain the Premises in a habitable condition and comply with applicable Arkansas habitability standards (Ark. Code Ann. § 18-17-601 et seq.). Landlord shall maintain structural components, roof, plumbing, heating, electrical systems, and supplied appliances, except where damage is caused by Tenant's misuse, negligence, or failure to report issues.

5.2 Tenant's Responsibilities

Tenant shall:

  1. Keep the Premises clean and sanitary;
  2. Properly dispose of trash in provided containers on scheduled collection days;
  3. Replace batteries in smoke detectors as needed and test detectors monthly;
  4. Promptly report maintenance issues through approved channels (see Handbook);
  5. Be responsible for damage caused by Tenant, occupants, guests, or pets beyond normal wear and tear;
  6. Maintain yard areas as agreed, including keeping the enclosed porch area tidy;
  7. Not make alterations without written consent.

Article 9 — Default & Remedies

9.1 Events of Default

Tenant shall be in default if any of the following occur:

  1. Failure to pay rent or other charges when due;
  2. Violation of any material term of this Lease or the Handbook;
  3. Abandonment of the Premises;
  4. Providing false information on the rental application;
  5. Illegal activity on the Premises;
  6. Unauthorized pets or occupants;
  7. Failure to pay the security deposit prior to move-in.

9.2 Remedies

Upon default, Landlord may pursue all remedies available under Arkansas law, including but not limited to: (a) termination of this Lease; (b) eviction proceedings; (c) recovery of unpaid rent and charges; (d) recovery of damages; (e) collection costs and attorney's fees where permitted by law. Landlord may remove abandoned property in accordance with Arkansas statutes.

9.3 Holdover

If Tenant remains in possession after the Expiration Date without a written renewal, tenancy shall become month-to-month upon the same terms, except rent may increase upon thirty (30) days' written notice. Holdover rent may be charged at 150% of the monthly rent until Tenant vacates.

9.4 Early Termination

Early termination by Tenant before the Expiration Date requires Landlord's written consent and may require payment of an early termination fee, forfeiture of deposit, and continued liability for rent until the Premises are re-rented, to the extent permitted by law.
